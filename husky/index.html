Husky Checkout

Redefining the equipment checkout experience

Objective

Husky Checkout is an app that allows students to check out IT equipment quickly and efficiently to save time for students and staff. In collaboration with the University of Washington-Bothell (UWB) IT department, we identified ways to improve the checkout system by removing the paper checkout process and developed an electronic system.

Challenge

What were the challenges that the staff and students faced with the last system? How can we improve the overall equipment checkout process so that it is less time consuming and tedious?

To understand the current checkout system, we completed a contextual inquiry by shadowing the IT staff and observing the checkout process for the faculty and students. Based on our research, we developed a journey map to understand what students and staff need to complete the checkout equipment.

Formative Study and Insight

To further our understanding, we created cultural probe kits for the staff to track their thoughts and feelings about the check out process for 7 days. We analyzed their feelings and conducted follow up interviews based on their responses.

Click Here to view an in-depth analysis of the cultural probe.

Cultural Probe Kit Findings

We found that the staff enjoys their job, but find it stressful when many people are trying to check out equipment at the same time.
Many staff reported that they did not like repetitive tasks and documentation with the current checkout system as it leaves room for human error.
We found that stress doesn't correlate with their work. The stress associated with time as the staff was a lot more stressed towards the end of the week.

Interviews

We interviewed 3 different UW IT Department staff that completed our cultural probe kits to learn more about their motivations and pain points.

Developing Husky Checkout

Based on our findings from the cultural probe kit and interviews, we intend to create a software application to minimize the staff's repetitive nature of the work to make checking out equipment quicker to reduce some of the stress associated with it.

Paper Prototype

We created a paper prototype to test the user flow of the app with other students in the class. We found that giving users the option to customize was unnecessary and we focused on the student's experience using the app.

Final Prototype

Checking out equipment should be painless

User need: User needs to get recommendations about different equipment so they can pick equipment that fits their needs.

User need: User needs to feel familiar with a new interface so they do not feel overwhelmed using the app.

Responses: Users can check out equipment with fewer clicks compared to the previous iteration of the checkout system. Users are familiar with shopping commerce websites by adding items to cart to check out equipment (think Amazon). The catalog on the app removes out of stock items and simplifies the information about the equipment. Lastly, the final interaction has an email notification for students to know when items are due so staff can predict their equipment inventory.

Next Steps

Develop analytic measurements system within the app to help inform the design refinements of Husky Checkout feature that will better serve the user needs.

Test the prototype of the app with students to see if it reduces the amount of stress and repetitive nature of UW IT Department staff.
